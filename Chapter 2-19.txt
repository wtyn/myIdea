Second death reported in growing measles outbreak - The Washington Post

Health officials say the outbreak has been traced to at least two children in Virginia, who have not been vaccinated for measles.

In Fairfax County, officials said the health department has been notified of at least two children who have been confirmed to have been exposed to measles in the area, which is in the city where the outbreak began.

In Richmond, officials said a 3-year-old boy, who was hospitalized with a measles infection, passed away from the infection earlier this month at the age of 3 months.

Officials have confirmed that the child is a 7-year-old girl, and that the child is a 3-year-old boy.

Officials say that the child's parents were also identified, and that the child has been vaccinated.

The state Department of Health in Charlottesville said it has notified Fairfax County Health Department and the Virginia Department of Public Health.
