A top judge issued an order that Friday halting the deportation of 6,000 undocumented immigrants living in the United States.

The decision came one day after the Supreme Court said it could hear the case of a man who was deported from his native Argentina in 1986 because his family was in the United States illegally.

"The government has failed to provide justice and fairness to those who are affected by the illegality of the deportation of these individuals," Justice Elena Kagan said in a court filing.

"We continue to stand with those who have lost their homes, families, livelihoods and will never return to be able to provide for themselves and their children."

U.S. District Judge James Robart issued the order because of the long history of deportation that has plagued the country.

The case is the latest in a string of cases under review by the court.

In 2008, the first case of its kind in the United States was in New York, when a Mexican immigrant from the United States was brought to the United States.

In the case, a U.S. judge ordered that a "deportation order be issued to the alien's name and address only if there is proof that the alien was the subject of a crime or if there is a reasonable suspicion that the alien was a threat to society."
